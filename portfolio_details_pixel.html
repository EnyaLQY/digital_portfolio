Pixel Painting Robot


Project information

  • Category: Physical Design & Prototyping
  • Timeline: 2022.9 - 2022.12
  • Team members: 4
  • Position: Designer, Programmer
  • Developing Tools: Digital Drawing (Photoshop, Procreate), Cardboard, 3D printing, Laser Cutting, Hardware (motors, sensors, etc.), Software (Fusion 360, PrusaSlicer, Inkscape, Arduino)
  • Video Link: short video demo

Design Description

Our project is focusing on creating a robot that can provide an abstract painting according to the given picture. Users could provide the robot with their selfie or any picture to work on, the robot will automatically transfer it into a pixel image and paint it on a designed draw pad. The transferred pixel picture will be provided instead of transferred by the robot itself. Also, the color will be limited to 3 and the drawing pad will be designed no larger than 8 by 8. (Draw pad sizes are subject to change.)

Initial Design

After receiving feedback from our professor and TAs, our group redesigned the style of the robot with reference to the 3D printing machine. (Illustrations by Qianya (Enya))

We discussed the main functioning parts of the robot. Link to detailed design documentation. The implementation of this robot can be described into 4 functional parts that work together to create a pixel painting:

(a) Z-axis moving unit which is the printing bed, and it can also move along the Y-axis by motor and belt

(b) X-axis moving unit which is fixed at a certain height and can move along the X-axis by motor and belt

(c) Rotating color plate with 6 stamps of different colors & (d) Y-axis pushing unit which moves along the Z-axis and pushes the selected color stamp to stamp on the paper

Cardboard Prototype

Based on the design diagrams, we built a simple physical prototype in actual size with cardboard, tapes and chopsticks. During the discussion with faculty group, we kept improving the structure of the robot. The real proportional structure and components would better help us find out what parts we were missing so that we needed to model and 3D print on our own.

Link to more cardboard prototype pictures.

First Prototype

Our group divided up different tasks of the robot. Everyone was responsible to design and revise 3D printing or laser cutting components for different tasks. Then we assembled all the components which was our first demo prototype.


Each task was implemented with belt, pulleys, motor, and 3D printed components.

(a) Z-axis moving unit

(b) X-axis moving unit

Final Prototype

After the first prototype presentation, we reflected our project and made a plan about what we should do next:

  • Add a supporting board between z-axis rail and drawing pad
  • Change the stamp style and spring to make printing easier
  • Integrate wiring connections
  • Code implementation for finding the accurate position to paint
By completing these tasks, we polished our final working prototype, which can select colors and find the correct position to paint pixel patterns.
