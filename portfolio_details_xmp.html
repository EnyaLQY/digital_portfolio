XMP Website


Project information

  • Category: UX Research & Web Development
  • Timeline: 2023.1 - 2023.5
  • Client: Cornell Law School
  • Team members: 7
  • Position: UX Reseacher & Designer, Team Coordinator
  • Developing Tools: Figma

Project Goal

Our project introduces the Xenophobia Meter Project (XMP) which aims to inform academics, non-governmental organizations, and the general public on levels and changes in xenophobic Twitter speech. The project challenges governments that ignore and/or engage in xenophobic speech as well as increases public awareness of Xenophobia issues. We will create a user-friendly website utilizing the UI/UX design process and present a data visualization dashboard of the study results.

Team Setup

According to team members' expertise, our team has been divided into two sub-teams: UX Design and Technical Development. As a person who has experience with UX design and programming, I will work in the UX team for market research and user research during the first half of the project. Then I will shift to the technical team to help with coding data visualization and website development.

We had a team policy that we will rotate our roles as the meeting lead and note-takers for weekly supervisor check-in meetings and client meetings. To ensure cohesive teamwork and consistent documentation, I take responsibility as a team coordinator, organizing all materials including meeting notes, meeting agenda, and project schedule.

Research & Information Hierarchy

  • Market Research & Field Study

    We did market research with similar projects in xenophobia and hate speech for inspirations. Besides, I learned more about the XMP project by running a field study at the virtual calibration meeting in Law School. I observed how the labeling process worked, asking questions regarding the actual meter, how the tweets were selected and getting initial feedback on data visualizations we had based on our market research from labelers.

  • Labeling Experience Analysis

    I reviewed the XMP ICWSM 23 paper and labelers’ interview transcript, and created a new affinity diagram in FigJam for labeling experience. As one of our goals is to present the labeler's human effort, I categorized my findings into three sessions which are Recruitment and Training, Labeling Procedure, and Challenges, Strategies, and Growth of the Labelers.

  • Information Hierarchy

    Based on the research analysis and clients' needs, we brainstormed the content genres on the website and decided the information hierarchy.

Prototype Design

The prototype design had three phases: Low-fidelity, mid-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes.



Low-Fidelity: Cornell Red

To quickly visualize content ideas and gain early feedback from clients, the UX team created low-to-mid fidelity screens. This iteration used iconic Cornell red with the first version of content designed by the UX team.




Mid-Fidelity: Visual Exploration

After recieving the feedback from clients, the UX team members explored different visual styles for typography, colors, and elements. Consulting with Cornell Branding Office, we would like to explore alterative colors and styles other than Cornell red. Finally, the yellow-themes (designed by me) was selected by the client.




High-Fidelity

After gaining feedback from clients and deciding on the visual style, the UX team created all Hi-Fi screens with uniform visual elements and typography. The prototype was wireframed with the revised content for the user testing.

User Testing

The high-fidelity prototype was handed to the development team for the code implementation. Meanwhile, to identify the unnoticed problems and improve the prototype, I conducted the usability test with 5 participants (different nationalities, religions and professions) at Cornell. The evaluation criteria of the usability testing includes effectiveness, efficiency, error rate and objective experience.

Considering we have a broad variety of audience including the general public, XMP team, researchers, non-government organizations and government agencies, it is challenging to accept all the user feedback and fulfill everyone’s needs. Our goal is to give the user the dataset and relevant information without biasing and give them the chance to judge whether what we are doing is right or wrong. So we should keep the objectivity of building a neutral, informational academic website. Therefore, we should prioritize the readability of the website to make them more accessible. To improve this, we changed the font size and added videos for different experiences other than plain text reading.

To Increase efficiency of the website, we changed the navigation bar by getting rid of the button and adding indicators to highlight the page the audience are on. Besides, we could emphasize Cornell credential by adding founder acknowledgement and changing the logo. These are powerful signs to highlight the association with Cornell and strongly defend our project.

The changes would be applied to both the Figma prototype and the actual website.

Website Implementation + Final Deliverables

The actual website is in the process of being handed off and will be available soon.
