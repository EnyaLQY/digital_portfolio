UX Research - Long-distance Friendship


In this project, our team aimed to refine the tricky problem about maintaining long-distance relationship. We did literature review of 15+ paper to justify the importance of best friendship. In order to narrow down our scope and discover unique insights, we conducted two rounds of design research:

  • Diary Study:
    A week-long diary study which combined the elicitation (helped track repetitive activities and capture the experiential elements in the moment) and feedback (provided more in-depth insights into the user's motives) techniques.

  • Cultural Probes:
    Provided cultural probes to provoke inspirational responses and unique values from participants.

Based on the related work and study findings, we designed an experience that allows long-distance best friends in adulthood to stay connected with each other across the physical and psychological distance by creating a customizable alarm clock with an app that amplifies laughter and spontaneity within the relationship.

Case Study - FindFood App

The objective is to redesign the FindFood App, with a focus on increasing utilization (number of bookings / user) among existing users, avoiding user churn and improving users’ shopping experience.

Based on the market research and given user research, I anaylzed the key existing problems with FindFood App:

  • There are still too many options with the current filter feature for the users, which is overwhelming.
  • Managing the favorite restaurants (“Favorite” tab) is less efficient.
  • The restaurant highlights do not provide all necessary information for decision making.
  • Users might change their mind at the last minute or forget their reservations.

This mini project took two days, with the [improved Figma prototype] and a [report] as final deliverables.
